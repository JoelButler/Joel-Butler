Origami Koi

Is it a skill? I’m not sure. It was actually really close to being a huge waste of time. While exploring Michaels, I stumbled upon an origami book by Won Park which contained an incredibly realistic dollar bill Koi fish.

As a child, my sister and I folded countless paper cranes. She even folded around 2000 miniature cranes as some sort of sick twisted mural art project. Each crane takes about a minute to fold. In any case, I absolutely had to try my hands at this a koi fish! How hard could it be?

Well, for two hours of fun (or pain depending on how you look at it) all you need is an American dollar, two hands, and a little bit of patience. There two distinct moments where I was close to ripping the dollar into a million pieces. Once during steps 15 through 30 which involved 45 minutes or so of pre-folding. And once again during step 38 because how am I supposed to understand what’s happening there?!
