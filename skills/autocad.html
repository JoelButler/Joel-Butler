LIFTING ASSEMBLY

As part of the projectâ€™s installation design and process, a snatch block was chosen to raise an object weighing up to 16 tons. The selected 20 ton snatch block makes use of a shackle to connect itself to other objects. For this project, a new lifting apparatus was required in place of the shackle.

A simple design made of readily available and easy to manufacture material was required. The chosen design links the 16 ton body to the snatch block by means of two pins and two plates. Based on the required size of the shackle pin, it is clear that the smaller pin connection is the weakest point in the design. Therefore, engineering calculations of shear stress, bearing stress, and normal stress, were carried out at this location to determine if the design meets the required factor of safety.

Engineering calculations of shear stress, normal stress, and bearing stress were carried out on the diagrams above. After confirming the lifting device met the safety requirements for the project, the design was sent to a professional structural engineer for final approval and stamping.
