DESK

I wanted a space where I could study, surf the internet, house my electronics and play games, but, I couldn’t find anything I really liked in any stores. So, I opted to build my own.

After I completed the design on paper, I started cutting some birch plywood and maple. It’s a fairly simple design but has removable, adjustable shelves, an open back for ventilation, and additional support.

Birch plywood and solid maple were the two basic materials I used for the project.

I used a table saw, miter saw, nail gun, and planer to work on this project. Finally, I stained and sealed the desk so that it would stand up to some abuse. It took quite a while to put this together but I have always been very happy with the results.

WOODEN CLOCK

I have had an interest in watches for quite some time. I bought my first in 2006 and still wear it frequently. I also think that mechanical clocks are really neat. This is where this project started for me.

To see if this was something I would enjoy, I began researching wooden clocks. Mechanical clocks are interesting works of art that take patience, and precision to build.

Eventually, i'd like to design my own. The CNC skills I have gained will hopefully link everything together nicely.

For now, I purchased a cheap scroll saw from craigslist and started cutting out gears by hand. It’s a very tedious process, but also very relaxing. Hopefully when i’m done, this clock will tick away loudly.
