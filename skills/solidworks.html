SAE FUEL TANK

As part of the UVic formula SAE team, via correspondance, I designed, the race car’s fuel tank. This fuel tank was used on the formula SAE race car for the 2015 season event in Michigan.


Technical specifications and documents were used to create the fuel tank focusing on space, size, material, manufacturing and existing physical constraints. Based on these design restrictions, detailed sheet metal drawings were produced specifically designed for ease of manufacturing.

DOUBLE GEAR MOTOR

The double gear motor assembly uses two small electric motors to drive independent axles. It was used as the drive system for small robots and the independent axles allow the robot to change directions.

All components were measured from a physical model using a digital caliper. The assembly contains quite a few unique components.


The double gear motor uses small electric motors and gears to drive two independent axles. They are used for powering small “sumo” robots.

STERLING ENGINES

Sterling engines are insanely cool. They use heat as a fuel source. Air displacement caused by a temperature difference drives the engine. Warmed air is displaced by cool air. The heated air cools, and the process repeats. This continual heating, displacing, cooling, and heating moved a piston and turns a wheel. If manufactured correctly, these engines will actually run off of the heat of your hand!


The Sterling engine below is designed to be powered by a small tea candle or flame. Anything that can be used to create a temperature difference can be used to power these engines including ice.



From these 3D models, assembly drawings and detail drawings can easily be produced for part manufacturing and assembly.
